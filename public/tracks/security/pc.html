PC Security

Introduction

When using your PC to complete tasks, it is important to protect your information to make sure that your computer is not vulnerable to attack from outside attacks.

Software best practises

The below recommendations detail the importance of keeping you software updated and installing an antivirus program:

  1. Keep your computer up-to-date - Those pesky Windows Updates that always seem to restart your PC at the most inconvient time are extremly important for keeping your PC safe and secure. While it can be tempting to hit the 'Install Later' button, these updates contain the latest security patches to fix current security vulnerabilities within the Windows Operating System
  2. Use Anti-virus and Anti-Malware - The use of antivirus software help protect your computer against most viruses, worms, Trojan horses and other unwanted computer viruses. There are many options available both free and paid for, at the very least, you should enable the Microsoft's Windows Security which does a pretty good job at protecting your computer

Device best practises

Below is a list of best pracitises that will help keep your PC safe:

  1. Never leave your PC unattended - Never leave your PC unattended, especially in a public place. If you do ever leave your PC unattended, always lock your PC using the Windows shortcut: Windows-Key + l
  2. Backup your data - Always back up your data to a location different from your PC. Use something such as an external hard-drive and a cloud-based solution such as Google Drive. This will make it easy to recover your data if your PC ever experiences a technical fault.
