Mobile Security

Introduction

With the increasing number of people who use their mobile devices on a daily basis, there are a number of nefarious who try to take advantage of people's mobile devices. This section will outline some of the best practises which you can follow to protect your mobile device while you are out on your travels.

Use authentication

Most modern mobile device have some form of authentication such as fingerprint scanners, facial recognition and pin verfication. These authentication features are designed to protect your devices from unauthorised access and can also help protect your mobile device if you ever lose your phone. Where possible, use two-factor authentication which is an extra layer of security. This can consist of using a PIN and a fingerprint to access your phone.

Avoid public Wi-Fi

Avoid using public Wi-Fi as they tend to be extremely insecure. Although it's very tempting to use that free Wi-Fi at the coffee shop or hotel lobby, don't do it. There are many tools available online which even a novice hacker can use to intercept traffic flowing over a public Wi-Fi. This is known as a a man-in-the-middle attack where a hacker sits in between the user and the end destination to access, relays and possibly alters the communications. This allows the hacker to eavesdrop data being transfered and gain access to sensitive data.
